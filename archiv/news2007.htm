3rd June 2007: Some Updates

Well, as mentioned in my latest report, the Fleet Command Reports will get a bit rarer these days. This one should have been release almost a week a ago, but i didn't found time for the lastest medals of the campaign. Hopefully finding some time for that in the next days.

So the campaign is over. Pretty much a sudden end, usually I have concepted an additional chapter with a major strike against Horus, but the time the campaign took has been pretty long now, and after some time there is some need for a break, to give out medals and start new RPGs where members can play with their ranks they have earned meanwhile and new members can join easier. Captain Skywaler had asked the council end of 2006 for starting an own Fleet Mission Run on, but this got skipped till after the campaign, so I guess it's time for him to start now. Also if there are applications for the second FMRO please contact me or one of the other council members to bring it up to the council. You could add a small description what the story is about.

Well what is a Fleet Mission Run On, for those who have joined us in the last few months. Well a Fleet Mission Run On is a Run On with a limited time, 2 to 3 months, there are medal sets given out for the participiants and the FMROs need to get green light from the council.
A Fleet Mission Run On should be designed for everyone in the fleet to take part. Well it's not nessesary that every ship needs to take part in the storyline. So if the captain of a ship wants to take part that don't automatically means the entire ship is part of the mission, but there should be a way to include each person in a FMRO, maybe a visitor, of observing Jedi, or temporal transfer or whatever, so everyone in the fleet has the possibility to play in the FMRO from the beginning on.

Order of Jedi

Well cause the Jedi are pretty popular in the last time I would like to write a few lines about it. When the 5th fleet started we didn't have a Jedi Subgroup or Sith Subgroup, not even when we turned sides. At some point the Order of Jedi got build not to give addtional activity to the fleet. It has never been concept of the subgroups to divide the fleet into several group that have seperated activities and are just losely affiliated with the other parts of the fleet. No the OoJ got build cause of the need that we would have got problems having every Ensign plays as Jedi Master. So the OoJ got build to have an overview on who has which Jedi title and later who has which force powers and so.
In the campaign it got pretty much visible, with a couple of Ensign Jedi Masters this could have been without real difficulty to play against the Phantoms.
Well, this is an RPG and if someone wants to play a Jedi Master he should be able to do it, the prblem is, most of the members don't act as lightsiders, some even worse then us ex-Sith. Also I'm expecting from Masters of the Jedi to act more wise then others.
Another point is that a battle with a Sith is the ultimate challange for a Jedi, at least it's like that in the movies, and that a Sith is usually a more deadlier fighter than a Jedi of the same level. I don't really expect someone here loosing a dual against a Sith, but add it as final combat in your RPG, and don't slice through them as if they were battle droids.
That's the reason for the padawanship, to learn how to play a Jedi like a Jedi.
Sure there have been problems with getting enough trainers for Padawans, but we have 3 trials running, maybe one comming soon. So there should soon maybe less problems for initiates to get padawan. I can also talk to the masters that those who were active in the initiates thread and/or are long time Initiate have a shorter padawan time then, but the master has the last decision on that.
Finally there is some change with the lightsabers. You can see on the profiles yourself. Knights can select a few different sabers now, Masters have some additional selections (Two sabers or Dual bladed saber). Something similar existed in the old roster, but meanwhile there is a form to change the lightsaber. You can access it through you member management page.
Have made single sabers to Knights and Dual saber or Set of two sabers to Masters cause I thought I remenbered the latest decision of the OoJ was something like that.
The changes on roster pages have been made a week ago, the pages for changing the sabers two days later, for those of you who have already mentioned that.

Well, I would like to speak on some write a small paragraph about it. In my oppinion the 3rd Outer Rim War didn't reached the quality of the earlier wars. Well, there were a couple of complaints about the earlier ways to submit scores and and the lack of a bot matching the right ppl together. There had been complaints on the scoring and the exeptions from the basic rules. Sure a couple of problems, but nothing too serious.
But what I specially dislike was, that the system didn't encourage players that aren't exactly top players to play in these tournaments, cause sooner or later they find out that loosing damages to position of their club in the battle and will stop playing. 1st February 2007: Outer Rim War III News

Outer Rim War III

The 3rd Outer Rim War has begun, well technically just this weekend, but I can almost smell burned TIE Fighter hulls, well hopefully not ours. IRC channel is #outerrim on undernet. Those of you that have played in the Outer Rim War before, know how things are going. You signup in there for a specified platform and for a team game or 1 on 1. You get matched against an opponent player that signed up for the same platform. You can make a game then, the winner gets reported. Both sides gain points for their teams, and you can signup for another match.

Counting in the 5th will be a bit different then in other clubs. You get one merit point for making a game and if you win the game you can get an additional merit point. There will be a table of merits you get getting specified amounts of merit points. There will be a tournament medal again everyone gets that participiates in the tournament. The difficulty for pilot wings will be increased: 20 games for silver pilot wings, 100 games for golden ones. Reason is, you have much more time to get a game. They kinda trippeled the time where you can signup for games since ORW2. In ORW2 weekends were 9 hours long, in ORW3 you have almost two complete days. Also the number of gamers should have increased. SO getting games and playing many games should be much easier then in the ORW2.

Medals of the ORW3 will be given out as always at the end of the tournament.

More news on ORW3: http://www.orw3.net

Ladder gaming is again part of the campaign, in a much more usefull way at the moment. Carriers have limited resources now. Lets take the Warspite for example. It has a cargo load or 1400 concussion missles, 1000 torpedos and 6 squadrons so 72 fighters/bombers. Well a torpedo run on an enemy target can cost 100 to 200 torpedos per squadron depending on the fighter/bomber type. If the Warspite sends 5 squadrons against an opponent target it will loose maybe 50% of the loaded torpedos.
Well after a battle a Carrier needs new figters and ammunition and resources are limited in the region where we are opperating. So each ship has an amount of resource points that can be spent on refilling weapons and fighters.
For solving Phase One ships get resource points, but also for getting it's members gaming in the ladder. Or for good results in the Short Story Competition, or for good results in the Gaming Night. Also, opponents get more dangerous in the later Phases of the campaign. There better weapons are required like Mag Pulse Torpedos or Snifflers.

Some changes on medals

Well there are some smaller changes on medals that will be done. Well the number of medals given out kinda increased in the last time for stuff besides gaming and roleplaying.
I have given out some performance medals in the last time or confimed such medal sugestings, and I have seen others giving out these medals as well. It's not my indication to flood newer members with lower medals. Even if the attraction might be higher, it didn't really led to a draft in the roleplaying or gaming sections and it's just not the style of the 5th. Better to give a more important medal after a time when officers earned it.
So performance medals will be given when officers leave a position (surely not in case of getting the next higher position, like leaving Assistant Director to get Director in the same subgroup. When someone leaves his position he usually gets a general honor medal for the work he did into his job, sure the importance of the position and the length he did it are important, but especially in lower positions the quality is an important factor too. In case of positions in the OoJ Jedi Honor medals can be given as well instead of general honor medals in and in the NRN the Crimson Award for News and Informations.

Blue Medallion
The Golden cycle had a higher value before. To restore the value again the Golden Cylcle will be splitten into two medals: The Golden Cycle remains the higher one and the Blue Medallion will become the lower version. To make sure the Blue Emblem don't gets massively given out in the next medal term, the medal can't be awarded more then two times by a single officer in the first medal term of 2007.

Leadership Award
The Leadership Award is an aged medal that has been given out in the first Inter Club Tournaments but haven't been used in the last years. I have thought on reviving it for the Run On scene. So the Game Master of a Fleet Mission Run On can give out this medal now to the best player in a Run On. This medal is given additionally to the Progress Coin the player gets but the game master can't award this medal to himself
(By the Way there won't be given a extra medal for the game masters anymore. The get a Progress Coin like the best players in their Run On. I guess most officers that have enough experiance for Game Mastering a FMRO have meanwhile enough Golden Cycles anyway.)

Jedi Honor Medal
The Jedi Honor Medal will be given out for a concret reason now. Will be given to a padawan that finishes his training. It can be given to the master that trained the padawan as well. The medal can also be given out if an officer retires from a position in the Jedi Council. Other resons for giving out this medal are reserved to the Fleet Command Council.
The Jedi Honor Medal is indeed a bit low medal to give it to a Padawan after several month of training, but don't forget the Padawan gains the title of Jedi Knight with the corresponding rights for completing the training, the medal is just a bonus.

Crimson Award for News and Informations
This medal will be given out for the 1st time an officer releases 3 issues of the Newsletter or is represented in at least 6 issues with at least one quality article. You can only award the CANI one time for this reason. Means, you don't get 2 CANIs for 6 released issues, but you can get additional CANIs for other reasons and the CANIs you got before don't count in here.
This rule counts back till the Halloween Edition of the Fifth's Exploit, so Commodore Wolverine gets the CANI for realeasing the 3 issues below.
The CANI can also be given when officers in higher positions of the NRN leaving their position after a long time, usually not for members retireing from lower positions and also not for ppl that aren't too long in the NRN.
The Fleet Command Council surely reserves to give out the medal for other issues related to the NRN.

Inter Club Training Event
Well, recently got contacted from Sith Warrior Karel Bruth`Kothae from the Dark Jedi Brotherhood.

The ICTE takes places saturdays one undernet in the channel #outerrim. Most of the actual Star Wars games are played there, Jedi Outcast, Jedi Academy, Battlefront I/II, Empire at War ..., but make sure you have downloaded the newest official Lucasarts patch for the game you want to play.
