17th October 2006: Fleet Command Report

You know I haven't been so much online in the last week cause real life kept me busy in the last time, ... as usual.

Well there have been a tie in the voting for the next Run On between Behind the Shades Chapter III (Rear Admiral Mattias Gustafsson) and Black Plague (Captain Talon Jade).
Instead of making a new vote I thought on starting both Fleet-Wide Mission Run Ons to give as many members as possible the chance to game master these run on missions so they are able to create their very own part of the 5th fleets history much more then they cn do as player of a run on. Sure it's a good chance for the career as well.

Having two run on missions at the same time will be a quite difficult task for players and game masters.
The fleet wide run on missions are usually more difficult then a normal run on. They have a time limit of 2 to 3 months so at the end of the time medals can be given out. One of 3 possible medals will be given out, a Medal of Communication for participation, the better players get a Good Performance Award instead and the best players get the Progress Coin. Cause of the time limit game masters need to proceed the story, even when the players aren't that far at a specified time.
With two fleet wide run on missions at the same time it will be a bit more difficult to ensure the continuity. Captain Talon Jade's Run On plays in the timeline after Rear Admiral Mattias Gustafsson's Run On. So, in case someone gets promoted while the run on, this promotion can't be announced in Tax' run on cause he may have started in Talon's Run On, which plas later in the time line with the old ranking, same counts for destroyed ships or so. Or an ensign that gets newly asigned to a squadron, can write how he joins the squadron in Tax' run on, but in Talon's Run On he has to do a if he would have been in the squadron already.
Well, I'm really curious to see if this project will suceed and if it isn't to complicated for the players to play in two different time frames at the same time. At least this will give roleplayers a lot of possibilities for gaming, especially with the time. For example a player could start in Talon's Run On with an injury and writes in the middle of Tax' Run On how he got it, or force sensible characters may have a vision in Tax' Run On of a scene that happens in Talon's run on.

The ORW3 is still unclear. Most of you already read that Rear Admiral Lycah Fox, Rebel Squadrons Fleet Commander recently retired, so we wasn't sure what happens to the ORW3 project.
Got a mail from Major General Jon Anchorage two days ago. The veterans in here may remember him as Captain Jon Anchorage who played massivly for the 5th fleet in the 1st Outer Rim War. Well, it looks like the Rebel Squadrons haven't given up the project, nevertheless the Republican Diplomatic Corps need to put up connections with the RS again to see details on the progress of the project and see if they need help from our side.
Meanwhile there are also ideas from your favorite High Command Council to manage an own Inter Club tournament.
I know many members are waiting for a long time now for the next Inter Club tournament, but organizing such an event isn't a simple task, especially when so many different clubs are involved.

27th September 2006: Short Fanfiction Competition

The Basic Premise:
All members of the New Republic Fifth Fleet are welcomed to write a short fanfiction based on the Star Wars universe. Stories can be set in any pre-existing Star Wars era (such as the prequel era, the original trilogy era, expanded universe era, and so forth). Stories may include the Fifth Fleet, but are not necessarily required to do so. The stories will be kept private until the due date, when they will be published online for all Fleet members read and rate via private voting system. The authors of the three highest rated stories will be handsomely rewarded.

The Specifics & Rules:
1. Stories must be fanfiction set in a pre-existing Star Wars universe era. No introducing new major content such as new races, force abilities, etc.
2. No length requirements. Please notice that the event is called a short fanfiction competition. Also consider what having an excessively long story or a paragraph long story will do to its rating.
3. Story plot and writing must be completely original. You may not borrow any plot content or writings from another author. Quotations of what an existing character has said in the past are acceptable.
4. All stories should be kept private until the rating begins. For the sake of your own creative privacy, do not share your stories early.
5. Edit and revise your story. Each member is only allowed to enter one story, so once you submit your story, it is final. No changes will be allowed. Stories will be posted on an as-is basis. I will not fix spelling or grammar issues, and I will not publish a story that grammatically or intelligently embarrasses the Fleet.
6. Stories should be e-mailed to Fleet Admiral Matt Sifi before October 11, 2006. If a story is not e-mailed by October 11, 2006, then it will not be added to the reading and rating list.
7. Once the stories are posted for reading, you must read them all if you plan to rate them. To rate one story, you must rate all of the stories and therefore you must read them all. You may not, however, rate your own story. (Example: I read all of the stories submitted. I rate all of the stories submitted, except my own story.)
8. Once the rating period is over, the results will be posted. The author of the highest rated story will be awarded the Golden Tournament Medal. The author of the second highest rated story will be awarded the Silver Tournament Medal. And the author of the third highest rated story will be awarded the Bronze Tournament Medal.
9. Above all, have fun doing this. Enjoy embellishing yourself in the Star Wars universe. Take time to relax and tune in to your creative and passionate instincts.

Reference Data:
* Due Date: October 11, 206
* Send completed stories to: matt@mindblock.net
* Completed story formats: .doc, .rtf, .txt, .htm, .html, .xml, .odt, .sdw
* Questions: Post it on this thread or contact Fleet Admiral Matt Sifi
* Rating Period: October 11, 2006 through October 18, 2006
* Results Announcement: October 19, 2006

10th September 2006: Forum Guidelines

New Republic Fifth Fleet Forum Guidelines

All posts should be constructive, coherent, easily intelligible, and on topic.

Post in the correct forum and thread. Take your time to look at other topics and see where your post should go. If your topic is placed in the wrong forum, it will be moved or deleted by a moderator.

Proper spelling, grammar, and punctuation are necessary for accurate communication and is expected from all users.

Posts should be used as a general notification or discussion with a broad range of users, not for private discussions with another user. Posts can be directed toward a user or an audience of users, but in such a case it should be for the benefit of the whole community. Use email, private message, or another medium for one on one discussions.

Make obvious identification of your post’s subject matter in the subject blank.

English is the recommended language for community usage.

Politeness is usually a plus. It is possible, even advisable, to get your point across without acting out.

Personal attacks on other users (or non-users) either in public forums or private message is unacceptable.

Posting the same message multiple times or recreating a deleted thread is unacceptable.

Advertising and/or promoting a company, product, group, or affiliation for personal profit is generally frowned upon.

Spamming is not permitted; please keep all your posts as constructive as possible.

Member accounts that do not confirm their registration via the email link will be automatically deleted after 24 hours. Allow emails from forum_AT_mindblock_DOT_net to ensure the arrival of the registration email. Members should only register one account.

There will be no racial, ethnic, or gender based insults or discrimination on this forum. The New Republic Fifth Fleet is a global community accepting members from any location, background, creed, or color without discrimination. Violation of this guideline will likely get you banned from the entire mindblock.net domain with little or no exception to review or reconsideration.

1st September 2006: Quick Update

Name Convetions
Cause a couple of ppl have problems with selecting names in the last time I would like to explain the point.
I guess the RPG members in the 5th fleet have less problems with that. But a couple of the gamers seem to have a bit a problem with this. The name is not only a unique idetifier for a member of the fleet, with name something is ment that can be used as name in RPGs and in announcements. Sure many members of the 5th fleet are more here for gaming, but even those may get mentioned in announcements, newsletters or the New Republican news or other things that are partly in character. It may also happy that those officers reach higher positions like Squadron Commander and Wing Commander and are at least mentioned in RPGs, Run Ons and fan fiction stories.

So what are the rules for names:
English Language Usually Names start with a capital Latin letter, followed by a couple of Latin letters in lower case. That may sound stupid, but after a number of members signing in in ascii art or with Clan tags it's nessesary to mention it. This don't means a Japanese name isn't ok, it just need to be written with english letters.
Unique The name you choosed no other members of the 5th should use.
No Star Wars Characters Names like Luke Skywalker and Han Solo shouldn't be taken, usually this rules isn't seen strict for less known Star Wars Characters. Also names of Characters from other movies are not ment with this rule, so it's ok if someone takes James Tiberius Kirk as name or so.
No Ranks Military ranks, Jedi titles or other positional titles shouldn't be part of names. Darth is somehow an exeption since it's seen more as name compount here.

25th June 2006: Recruiting Campaign

In the attempt to get the some ships of the 5th fleet active again. I guess around 4 or 5 or 6 years passed since the last recruiting campaign.
Well, how does the recruiting campaign work. Well officers that recruit new members can get direct promotions besides the usual medals.
Recruiting is not allowed in channels, message boards and gaming servers of other clubs. Recruiting is also not allowed in channels, message boards and gaming servers assosiated with other clubs (for example the #outerrim channel).
An additional text field have been added to the joining form where new recuits can fill in the officer that recruited them.
The recruiter gets a point as soon as one of the recruits passes the Naval Academy exam.
Here is a overview about the promotions for recruits:
1 recruits EN to 2nd LT (1 week)
2 recruits 2nd LT to LT (1 week)
3 recruits LT to LCM (1 week)
5 recruits LCM to CM (2 weeks)
8 recruits CM to CPT (2 weeks)
12 recruits CPT to CMD (3 weeks)
16 recruits CMD to RA (4 weeks)
22 recruits RA to VA (5 weeks)
28 recruits VA to AD (6 weeks)
40 recruits AD to FA (7 weeks)
(The time showes the minium time an officer is in his rank before he can get promoted to the next ranking. For example an officer got promoted to lieutenant commander one week ago and recruited 13 new members in the last week. He has to wait one week till he automatically gets promoted to Commander and 2 more weeks for Captain.
There are also given some medals for the number of recruited officers. Republican Support Award for each 4 Recruits, Republican Service Medal for 10 Recruits, Star of Alderaan for each 30 Recruits.
Recruiting entire clans or clubs:
Well in case an officer can bring an entire clan joining the 5th surely each member of the clan counts as recruit, in case he isn't already in the club.
The recruits of the last two weeks surely count for the Recruiting Campaign too, like Cadet Decot that has been recruited by Lieutenant Commander Walters, in case the cates pass the Naval Exam.
It's not sure when the campaign ends, but recruiting numbers can ever count partially for a promotion. Like if an officer has more then half reached the normal service time required for the next promotion and has more then half reached the recruits reached for the next ranking, he surely can get promoted to the next ranking puting both results together.

17th May 2006: New Fleet Manual

The new fleet manual has been put online. You can currently see it at:

http://www.newrepublic.de/manuals2/index.html

It didn't replace the current fleet manual cause the academy courses are still referencing on the old manual.
So the old Fleet Manual will stay until the courses have been modified so they are referencing the new fleet manual.

Where did it start?
Well, what has changed, primary we have added the Crew Status to the fleet rules as it was discussed in the High Command Council and on the Fleet Board before. So officers don't have to be asigned to squadron anymore and officers that are higher then Squadron Comanders can't be asigned to squadron anymore. The changing is primary reasoned to make RPGs easier, so that officers that serve on the bridge of a ship don't have to be asigned to fly in squadrons at the same time. The other reason is to prevent double positioning, so that junior officers have real chances to get into higher positions. Usually a Carrier Captain isn't a bad choise for commanding a Squadron since he is usually high decorated, has much of experiance and the squadron has higher chances to get veteran or elite status. But even when a younger officer has less experiance for commanding a squadron then the Wing Commander or the Carrier Captain or one of the other officers in high positions, the younger ones should have at least the chance on triing if they manage these position and I guess the officers in high positions don' really lose status in the fleet when they give up their secondary or tertiary fleet position. I guess in the future we will think on dropping these double positions completely, but when the restructuration in done I guess we can think on a different primary project first.

The first results.
Well, officers could be removed from squadrons that don't have other positions, but there need to be written something on the roster which position they have. So Crewmember and Deck Officer got added to the positions. Ok, someone asigned to a ship is a Crewmember, so adding Crewmember as position was pretty logical. But why has Deck Officer been added. Well Deck Officer is more thought for RPG purpose to display those officers that play navigation officers or ship doctors or characters like that. The Deck Officer don't has real funtions on managing a ship OOC, these positions are just there for RPG Purpose.
There was also a problem with promotion abilities of Wing Commanders. Besides the Flagships where we had several wings, Wing Commanders were able to promote the entire crew of a ship ecept of the Carrier Captain, cause in a ship that has one wing every officer was asigned to that wing. When many officers would have been made Crew Member or Deck Officer the Wing Commander wouldn't be able to promote these anymore since they don't belong to the wing anymore. So the promotion ability of the Wing Commander have been changed in that way that the Wing Commander can promote everyone on his ship below the Position of Wing Commander now. (Well, that isn't very serious since all promotions currently going through the High Command Council and there is ever someone that is able to promote, but there were no intention to decrease the promotion ability of the Wing Commander so that has been adjusted)

Other Changings.
Well the Fleet Manual, the Code of Conduct and the Fleet Compendium have been put together to the new Fleet Manual. The double parts (For example there were a Ranking Chapter in the Fleet Manual and a Ranking Chapter in the Fleet Compendium) were put together. The army rank references and the German translations for the ranks have been dropped from the manual. I guess noone really cares for the German variants anymore and the army variants just caused irritations. The ID Lines moved from Fleet Rule to Fleet Guideline (Have been moved to the additional topics). Sure both decisions concern Fleet Rules so can it be changed that easily without Council voting and discussing it with the fleet members. For the skipping of the German variants and the Army variants, they are still Fleet Rules. Some needs to translate Captain with Kapitaen and not with GrossAdmiral or Leutnant. They are just not written in the Core Rules cause they are technically not used too often. For the ID Lines, well the construction of the ID Lines didn't change. Since noone in the fleet really takes ID lines too serious, I guess supposed the fleet is ok by that. There is also an optical change of the subgroup emblems. There are differen emblems for different positions within a subgroup now. Jedi wear their Lightsabers as sign for belonging to the Order of Jedi now. Padawans have default Trainings-Lightsabers, Jedi Knights and Jedi Masters default Lightsabers too, but they will be able to have indvidual sabers when the new roster gets done. On the current roster the lightsabers are placed in the Personnel Items slot. Fleet Positions have Icons now as could be seen in the Preview too. The icons are more thought as graphical element for the profile pages.

Comming Soon.
Well the next step in this progress will be adjusting the academy courses and redesigning the profile pages. I hope getting the roster done within the next weeks, but it may take longer.

Sure, this idea isn't new, but in the early times of the 5th when we have been basically a TIE Fighter clan such a structure wasn't really nessesary and the current structure was easier. Cause we are going more and more into Roleplaying there have been a couple of thoughts on changing this structure.

How would that change the structure in detail. Well basically everyone on a ship could be asigned to the Crew of a ship instad of being asigned to a specified position within a squadron. There are no limitations for the numbers of crew members asigned to ships. While a Small Calamari Cruiser can only have 2 squadrons so 24 pilots, it can have several hundret crew members.

Fleet Commander, Fleet Executive Officer, Fleet Senior Officer, Group Commanders, Carrier Captains and Wing Commanders automatically get crew status. I guess I don't need to explain that in a battle the place of the captain is on the bridge but something familiar counts for the Wing Commander. Not in each case it's better to organize the flight opperations of 72 fighter from a cockpit. For the RPG games and Run Ons Wing Commanders and officers with higher positions can still lead squadrons from their personnel fighter with ids like Red Zero or so.

Well a consequnce of this change would be that a couple of younger officers would get the chance on making the first step into the fleet administration, cause a couple of squadron command positions that are hold by wing commanders and officers in higher positions would get free.

Besides the officers in high ositions, which officers could have crew status too. Well that's basically up to the captains of the ships. Inactive members and reserves could be set as crew, as well as officers with admiral rankings that don't think they want to be pilots anymore, but basically that's up to the captain and the officers how they want to organize that. There is also a possibility to give those officers crew status that have ship titles like tactical officer or ship doctor.

This is one of a few concepts that went through the fleet command. But I don't know exactly when I'll implement it into the roster database, so there is some time for all members in the fleet to have the chance to protest against the changings in case they don't like them especially since the projects requires to change he basic fleet rules so each member has to be heard on that project.

1st March 2006: 8th Anniversary

8 years have passed in the life of the 5th fleet; some things changed, some things stayed the same.
The 5th fleet started as TIE Fighter club, went from X-Wing versus TIE Fighter and X-Wing Alliance to Jedi Knight. 2002 to 2003 we played massivly Jedi Outcats and Jedi Academy after that time. It's pretty pity that Battlefront didn't reach the popularity but I'm looking positive towards Empire at War.
We started in the Empire with a hand full of Imperium Stardestroyers before we build up Executor Class Superstardestroyers. TO show our superiority we build the Eclipse Class Superstardestroyer Dominance. But our arrogance brought us the destruction and we traveled through the community without destination. But out of the ashes of the Imperial 5th the Republican 5th fleet grown. Times passed where we started repairing our ships and our souls. When the Rebel Squadrons called us to the Outer Rim to fight their war against the empire again we returned into battle. Starting with the remnants of the Stardestroyers of the Imperial 5th we build up a new fleet with growing numbers of Mon Calamari Cruisers. After many years of battle we returned to a more quiter phase, but times may return where we shown our skills and our honor to our friends and our opponents.
